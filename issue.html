Depression

Depression is a big problem in Mexico City. Many people feel sad, tired, and hopeless for a long time. This can happen because of stress, work problems, family issues, or bad experiences. The city is very busy and crowded, which can make depression worse. Some people do not ask for help because they feel ashamed, but there are doctors and therapists who can help with therapy and medicine.

Anxiety

Anxiety is when a person feels very nervous, scared, or worried all the time. In Mexico City, many people have anxiety because of traffic, work pressure, money problems, or safety concerns. Some people get panic attacks, where they feel their heart beating fast and have trouble breathing. Relaxation exercises, therapy, and a healthy lifestyle can help people feel better.

Stress

Stress is common in Mexico City because life is very fast and busy. People work long hours, travel in heavy traffic, and deal with noise and pollution. This can make them feel tired, angry, or sick. Stress can cause headaches, sleep problems, and body pain. Taking breaks, exercising, and spending time with family can help reduce stress.
