Depression

Depression is a mental illness that makes a person feel very sad, hopeless, and tired most of the time. It can make it hard to enjoy life, do daily tasks, or even get out of bed. Some people with depression may also feel guilty, worthless, or have trouble concentrating. In serious cases, they might think about hurting themselves.

Depression is not just "feeling sad" for a short time—it lasts for weeks or months and affects a person’s whole life. It can be caused by stress, bad experiences, brain chemistry, or even run in families.

The good news is that depression can be treated with therapy, medication, exercise, and support from loved ones. If someone has depression, they should talk to a doctor or therapist for help.
